Building a Sumo Robot

Despite their size, sumo robots are complex machines. Building a sumo robot requires designing a system that blends sturdy mechanics with an efficient electronics setup. One must then control this system with a well thought out program.
Sumo robots usually are designed with either two or four rubber wheels, a metal chassis, and an angled slope on the front called a 'scoop' to wedge under an opponent.
Sumo robots are controlled by mircrocontrollers and use sensors to navigate because they are usually not remote controlled.
